## What Are the Most Underutilized Tabs3 Features?

Tabs3 includes extensive capabilities that most firms never implement. The highest-impact underutilized features include:

### Custom Field Architecture

Tabs3 allows creation of custom fields capturing firm-specific data: client billing requirements, matter budget allocations, insurance carrier codes, approval workflow status, and hundreds of other possibilities. Most firms operate with default field structures, losing the ability to track information essential for their specific operations.

Effective custom field implementation includes:

**Client Billing Requirements:** Fields documenting each client's specific billing preferences: invoice format, delivery method, payment terms, contact information, special restrictions.

**Matter Budget Tracking:** Custom fields tracking overall matter budgets, phase-specific allocations, amounts utilized, remaining balances, and variance alerts.

**Insurance Defense Codes:** UTBMS task codes, expense categories, phase codes, and carrier-specific identifiers enabling automated invoice formatting for insurance carriers.

**Workflow Status Indicators:** Fields tracking matter progression through internal workflows: pre-bill review status, final bill approval, invoice delivery confirmation.

The power of custom fields emerges when combined with automation rules. For example, configuring budget alert fields that trigger automatic notifications when matters approach budget thresholds transforms reactive budget management into proactive control.

Beyond Square One typically implements 15-25 custom fields for client firms based on practice area requirements and operational workflows. One insurance defense firm gained the ability to track and report on phase budgets across 500+ active matters through custom field implementation, transforming budget management from spreadsheet chaos to systematic control.

### Automated Workflow Rules

Tabs3's workflow automation capabilities allow creation of rules that trigger actions based on specified conditions. Common applications include:

**Time Entry Compliance Alerts:** Rules that identify attorneys with unrecorded time and automatically send reminder emails with specific metrics: "You have 4.5 hours of unrecorded time from yesterday. Please enter time today."

**Budget Threshold Notifications:** Rules monitoring matter budgets and automatically notifying responsible attorneys when utilization reaches 75%, 90%, and 100% of allocated amounts.

**Pre-Bill Review Automation:** Rules that automatically generate pre-bills and distribute to reviewing partners on predetermined schedules without manual intervention.

**Collections Follow-Up Sequences:** Rules triggering automated reminder emails based on invoice age: courtesy reminder at 15 days, formal notice at 35 days, escalation at 50 days.

**Data Validation Requirements:** Rules preventing invoice generation for matters missing required information: client billing codes, matter budgets, expense approvals.

Most firms operate Tabs3 with minimal workflow automation, relying instead on manual processes requiring billing staff to remember and execute routine tasks. This creates opportunities for oversight, delays, and inconsistency.

Systematic workflow automation eliminates these risks. One litigation firm implemented automated pre-bill generation and distribution, reducing billing cycle time from 14 days to 6 days by eliminating manual pre-bill preparation bottlenecks.

### Advanced Reporting Capabilities

Tabs3 includes extensive reporting functionality beyond standard aging reports and billing summaries. Advanced reports provide operational insights that inform strategic decision-making:

**Attorney Productivity Analysis:** Reports tracking billable hours, realization rates, collection rates, and write-off patterns by individual attorney.

**Practice Area Profitability:** Reports analyzing revenue, expenses, and profitability by practice area, identifying strongest and weakest performers.

**Client Profitability Analysis:** Reports calculating net profitability by client after accounting for write-offs, collection rates, and administrative costs.

**Budget Performance Tracking:** Reports comparing matter budgets to actual utilization, identifying patterns of chronic overruns or conservative estimates.

**Time Entry Compliance Metrics:** Reports tracking time entry lag by attorney, enabling targeted intervention for chronic late entry.

These advanced reports require custom configuration based on firm-specific needs. Software vendors provide report templates, but firm-specific insights demand customized report development.

Beyond Square One develops custom Tabs3 reports for client firms that surface actionable intelligence. One commercial litigation firm gained the ability to track attorney productivity across five dimensions simultaneously through custom dashboard reporting, informing compensation decisions and identifying performance improvement opportunities.

### Template-Based Invoice Automation

Tabs3 supports creation of client-specific invoice templates incorporating custom formatting, data fields, and layout specifications. This enables automated invoice generation that produces client-ready documents without manual formatting.

Most firms operate with generic invoice templates requiring manual customization for each client—a time-consuming process prone to formatting errors and inconsistencies. The manual approach becomes overwhelming when working with insurance carriers demanding specific invoice formats including UTBMS codes, phase budgets, and carrier-specific identifiers.

Template-based automation eliminates this manual work. For each client with specific requirements, create a template incorporating all necessary customizations. Invoice generation then produces properly formatted invoices automatically.

The template development requires upfront investment—typically 30-60 minutes per client template—but generates ongoing efficiency gains. One insurance defense firm working with 18 carriers implemented carrier-specific invoice templates, reducing invoice preparation time by 65% while eliminating formatting errors that previously caused carrier rejections.

## How Should Firms Approach Tabs3 Optimization?

Effective optimization requires systematic assessment and prioritized implementation rather than ad-hoc feature exploration. The recommended approach includes:

### 1. Operational Assessment

Begin with comprehensive analysis of current billing operations:

**Process Documentation:** Map existing workflows from time entry through invoice delivery and collections management, documenting all manual tasks and decision points.

**Pain Point Identification:** Survey billing staff and attorneys about operational frustrations, bottlenecks, and time-consuming manual tasks.

**Performance Baseline:** Establish current state metrics: billing cycle time, collection rates, AR aging, billing staff overtime hours, attorney time entry compliance.

**Technology Utilization Review:** Audit current Tabs3 configuration, identifying implemented features and unused capabilities.

This assessment creates a baseline understanding of current state and identifies highest-impact optimization opportunities.

### 2. Prioritized Implementation Planning

Not all optimization opportunities deliver equal value. Prioritize based on:

**Impact Magnitude:** Implementations eliminating significant manual work or solving critical operational problems receive highest priority.

**Implementation Complexity:** Quick wins requiring minimal configuration come before complex projects requiring extensive customization.

**Staff Readiness:** Changes requiring substantial training or workflow modifications need careful change management planning.

**Dependency Sequencing:** Some optimizations require prerequisite implementations—custom fields must exist before workflows can reference them.

Effective prioritization typically identifies 3-5 high-impact optimizations for initial implementation, creating visible improvements that build momentum for sustained optimization efforts.

### 3. Systematic Configuration Implementation

Execute optimizations systematically with thorough testing before production deployment:

**Development Environment Testing:** Implement configurations in test environment, validating functionality before production deployment.

**Documentation Creation:** Document all custom configurations, workflow rules, and report specifications for future reference and troubleshooting.

**Staff Training:** Train billing staff and relevant attorneys on new capabilities and modified workflows.

**Phased Rollout:** Deploy changes incrementally rather than simultaneous sweeping changes that overwhelm users.

**Performance Monitoring:** Track metrics demonstrating optimization impact: time savings, error reduction, process acceleration.

The implementation discipline separates successful optimizations from abandoned projects that create initial disruption without delivering sustained value.

### 4. Continuous Improvement Cycle

Optimization is not one-time project but ongoing process:

**Quarterly Review:** Regular assessment of operational performance identifying new optimization opportunities.

**Software Update Integration:** Tabs3 releases regular updates adding new capabilities—review and implement relevant enhancements.

**Staff Feedback Collection:** Systematic gathering of billing staff and attorney input about operational challenges and improvement ideas.

**Best Practice Adoption:** Stay current with legal billing industry best practices and corresponding Tabs3 configuration approaches.

This continuous improvement mindset ensures ongoing operational enhancement rather than optimization stagnation.
