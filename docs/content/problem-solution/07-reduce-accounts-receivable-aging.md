## What Is Accounts Receivable Aging?

Accounts receivable (AR) aging measures how long invoices remain unpaid after billing. It's calculated as: (Total AR ÷ Annual Revenue) × 365 = Average AR Aging in Days.

For law firms, AR aging directly impacts cash flow and working capital. Money sitting in aged receivables can't pay payroll, cover overhead, or fund growth initiatives. According to Beyond Square One, mid-size law firms (10-150 attorneys) typically maintain 60-90+ day average AR aging when 30-45 days represents healthy operations.

**Healthy AR Aging Benchmarks**:
- Excellent: <30 days average
- Good: 30-45 days average
- Warning Zone: 46-60 days average
- Problem: 60-90 days average
- Crisis: 90+ days average

## Why AR Aging Matters for Your Firm

### Cash Flow Impact

Every day of AR aging represents delayed access to your earned revenue. A firm with $5M annual revenue and 73-day average AR aging has $1M sitting in receivables. Reducing aging to 47 days releases $356K in working capital—money immediately available for operations.

### Opportunity Cost

Aged receivables create measurable opportunity costs. At 6% annual working capital rate, $500K in unnecessary AR aging costs $30,000 annually in lost opportunity. This compounds over time and represents real economic impact.

### Collection Difficulty

The longer invoices age, the harder they become to collect. Industry data shows:
- 0-30 days: 95-98% collection rate
- 31-60 days: 90-94% collection rate
- 61-90 days: 80-88% collection rate
- 90+ days: 60-75% collection rate

Every month an invoice ages beyond 30 days significantly reduces the likelihood of full collection.

## The 5 Strategies to Reduce AR Aging

### Strategy 1: Weekly AR Aging Review

**Problem**: Monthly AR reviews create 30-day gaps where aging problems compound undetected.

**Solution**: Implement weekly AR aging analysis and action planning.

**Implementation**:
- Generate AR aging report every Monday morning
- Review all receivables by aging category (30-45, 46-60, 61-90, 90+)
- Prioritize collection actions based on dollar amount and aging severity
- Track week-over-week improvement metrics

**Expected Result**: 15-20% reduction in average AR aging within 60 days through early intervention.

**Real-World Example**: A 45-attorney firm moved from monthly to weekly AR review and reduced average aging from 73 days to 56 days within 60 days—simply by catching problems earlier.

---

### Strategy 2: Tiered Collection Approach

**Problem**: One-size-fits-all collection communication treats all aging categories identically.

**Solution**: Implement tiered collection strategy based on aging severity.

**Tiered Framework**:

**30-45 Days**:
- Friendly email reminder with invoice attachment
- Payment portal link for convenience
- No escalation, maintain positive tone

**46-60 Days**:
- Phone call to client with specific invoice discussion
- Formal written notice via email and mail
- Identify any billing disputes or payment obstacles

**61-90 Days**:
- Partner involvement in collection communication
- Payment plan discussion and arrangement
- Clear expectations and escalation timeline

**90+ Days**:
- Final demand letter with specific deadline
- Collection agency consideration and communication
- Write-off evaluation for uncollectible amounts

**Expected Result**: Collection rate improvement from 88-90% to 94-96% through systematic escalation.

**Communication Scripts**: Professional, relationship-preserving language at each tier maintains client relationships while improving collections. Beyond Square One clients use scripted approaches that balance firmness with partnership mentality.

### Strategy 3: Accelerated Billing Cycles

**Problem**: Delayed time from work completion to invoice generation extends the entire collection cycle.

**Solution**: Reduce billing cycle time through process optimization and accountability.

**Implementation**:

**Move from Monthly to Weekly Billing**:
- Bill significant matters weekly rather than waiting for month-end
- Accelerates cash flow and reduces client sticker shock from large monthly bills

**Five-Day Post-Month-End Deadline**:
- Establish hard deadline for invoice generation: 5 business days after month-end
- Creates accountability and prevents billing delays

**During-Month Pre-Billing Review**:
- Conduct attorney pre-billing review during the month (not after)
- Eliminates end-of-month bottleneck

**Automated Time Entry Reminders**:
- Daily reminders to attorneys for time entry completion
- Prevents last-minute time entry rushes affecting billing quality

**Expected Result**: 15-20 day reduction in time from work completion to invoice delivery.

**Cash Flow Impact**: For a firm billing $500K monthly, 15-day acceleration provides $250K one-time working capital release plus ongoing cash flow improvement.

---

### Strategy 4: Payment Term Management

**Problem**: Accepting unfavorable payment terms without strategic consideration extends AR aging unnecessarily.

**Solution**: Implement strategic payment term policies and retainer requirements.

**Payment Term Policies**:

**Standard Net 30 Terms**:
- Establish Net 30 as standard expectation
- Consider early payment incentives (2% discount for 10-day payment)
- Require written approval for extended terms (Net 60, Net 90)

**Retainer Requirements**:
- Require retainers for all new matters
- Establish replenishment at 50% depletion (not exhaustion)
- Enforce retainer replenishment before continuing work

**Insurance Defense Leverage**:
- Use insurance carrier relationship leverage for faster payment
- Establish direct billing relationships with major carriers
- Implement systematic follow-up on carrier delays

**Expected Result**: Average payment terms improvement from 52 days to 38 days.

**Working Capital Impact**: 14-day improvement across $7M annual revenue releases $269K in working capital.
